    Chapter 1 · Break the intuition

    What does random really look like?

    Random does not mean neat, balanced, or alternating. Real randomness can look messy, streaky, and uneven in the short run.

    The coin has no memory

    After a long streak, many people feel the opposite side is “due.” It is not. Each flip is a new trial.

    Rule 2. The whole sample space has probability 1: P(S) = 1
    Rule 3. For non-overlapping events, we add probabilities: P(H or T) = 1

    Final synthesis

    What probability really means

    Chapter 1 builds the right mental model before moving to later topics.

    1
    Random does not mean neat or alternating. It often looks streaky.
    2
    Independent events have no memory. A previous streak does not force the next result.
    3
    An event is a set of outcomes from the sample space.
    4
    Probability measures how likely an event is, and in repeated trials it appears as long-run frequency.
    5
    Small samples can look misleading. Large samples usually stabilize around the true probability.
